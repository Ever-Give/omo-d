All she had left of her mother were drawings, paintings, stories, spell books and scrolls, and that statue of her mother in the center of town which was in line with 3 other statues of people she didn't recognize.

One hour was all it took to make a simple vegetable soup centered around potatoes, carrots, and a strange plant that looked similar to green beans, but was uniquely not found in the human world. With their special soup in their bowls, and the spoons made by the late wife of the household, the daughter followed her father to the four statues in the center of town.

"Before we begin" the father said, giving his daughter that look that said *you need to hear this*, "I should tell you that this is not a happy story, but it does have a happy ending."

Her ears and facial expressions settled somewhere between curious and confused. "How can it be unhappy but also be happy?"

"That, my little sunshine, is one of the greatest mysteries of this world."

As the man began, his daughter found herself so immersed in the story that no quotation marks or perspective descriptions were needed. It was one of those moments where a simple story made of words told an entire life's story, and the meaning stretched much further than simple words.

I met your mother only four days after the great shattering. She was lost, hungry, tired, and angry. Like many who had survived the tragedy, she had attempted to return home, only to find a smoldering crater where her hometown had been. You know of the great shattering, how it split the flow of magic into ley lines, how previous save zones had become smoldering craters, how entire cities had been reduced to debris... What you don't know, is what caused it.

At the time, she went by the name Roxella Steamlight. And was a wandering spirit known for her unique way of explaining the natural order in a way that made it easy for anyone to understand, even if they disagreed with her. She was also known for her incredibly dense magic charge, which created a core so potent that it seemed to create a glowing aura around her. She was only visible to those with the gift of sight.

Your mother met Roxella during a rare moment of instability. Roxella's normally perfectly stable core had been fluctuating, a response to the turmoil within her mind. A few hours prior, Roxella had performed a miracle at a nearby village, saving several people from certain death, and creating a stable supply of local food. This was the first time she had performed a blessing upon request.

When another nearby village heard of what had happened, they saught a blessing of their own, however, none of the people of that village possessed the sight. They never found Roxella, despite her being right there. Finding herself unable to help this starving village, her mind began to wander... What if she could have a body? Then everyone would be able to see her. Roxella petitioned a young spirit enchanter to create a body suitable of withstanding her power, then bind her soul to that body. The idea was simple: make Roxella available to everyone, not just the people that could see spirits.

Nobody knew that performing such a spell would destroy a boundary that had existed since the beginning of time, and create other boundaries that would last into eternity. When Roxy was bound to the prepared body, a connection was made between the world of the living, and the world of the spirits. These two worlds were fundamentally opposite, and the resulting energy ripple threatened to tear the worlds apart.

And so it began. Rivers of magic condensed into ley lines, cities crumbled, entire countries were reduced to smoke and ash, and the world began to shatter. The longer this continued, the worse it would get. Your mother was one of four great wizards that stopped this cataclysm from completely destroying the world. Once the tragedy had been brought to a stop, two things were obvious: first, Roxella was gone. Nobody knew where she went, or if she even still existed. Second, the damage was already done. No amount of wishing or spellcraft could restore the world to what it had once been.

That's when she appeared. A small, cat-like person with midnight blue fur, lavender eyes, and a presence that felt like more than just a person. The moment she appeared, she began crying. She cried for days, never growing tired, never hungry, never thirsty, just endlessly crying. Eventually, her tears dried up, and she began searching for something. She said she was missing a part of herself. When asked, she was never able to fully describe what part she was missing, only that she knew she was missing it. It took years to understand what was going on. This little creature was one of many children of the forest... A piece of what had once been Roxella.

Your mother spent her entire life following and helping this creature, and along this long and difficult journey, she grew closer to this creature, giving it the nickname of Roxy. She also began having visions of great disasters, along with ways to prevent them. She had gained a new and unique version of the sight. She used this to create prosperity, to usher in an era of peace, and to have a family, but it was not to be.

Your mother never realized that she too had been touched by the great shattering that split the world. Only she remembered the humans... Only she knew of the other world... The world that had been separated during the great shattering. When she settled down with me and we had you, the most wonderful thing that's ever happened to me, her core became distant. It was slow at first, random spells would misfire or fizzle out, she'd have a hard time remembering specific things, them it became pronounced. Within only 5 days, she had become bedridden. Her body was failing her. We tried everything. That's when we learned the price of being touched by the great shattering. When that power is passed down to a child, the mother can no longer exist.

Yep. She was wearing kitten gloves, or a more sturdy and also softer version of kitten gloves... How could they be both sturdier and softer? There was a sharp, pulling and tearing sensation as the glove was torn free.

To her right, it was daytime, bathing the meadow in a strange mixture of golden light and blue shadows... Hadn't her old teacher told her before about something that would happen only a few days after she experienced the calling?

As soon as she got a truly good look at him, he was gone, and Roxy was once again alone in the unending meadow.
